What does Biology Olympiad do?

Biology Olympiad is an academic club at Seven Lakes, geared towards preparing for national-leveled biology competition exams. Our primary goal is to prepare students for the U.S.A. Biology Olympiad Open and Semi-final exams. We hope that the enthusiasm for biology fostered within the club, combined with our helpful resources will drive students to achieve their goals.

The USABO exam consists of three rounds:



The first test, a 50-question Open exam encompasses general theoretical biology. This year, it will be administered between February 5th and 15th (tentative).

Next, the Semifinal Exam includes multiple choice problems, matching, and a free response to total between 120 to 180 points (varied). While there is no lab portion, this test applies practical analyses and reaches beyond the scope of general theoretical knowledge. The semi-final Exam is taken by students who score among the top 10% in the Opens and will be given between March 18th and 22nd (tentative).

The twenty top-scoring individuals on the semi-final exam are invited annually to UC San Diego for National Finals, a two-week training camp dedicated to practical and theoretical biology. The Final exam consists of test-taking and lab portions. Four finalists are then selected to represent the U.S. in the International Biology Olympiad.

How do I compete?

Register with us

Click here to join our club, and then go to the national USABO website here to register for the Open Exam. Additionally, join our Remind group by texting @7lbiooly to 81010 to stay on top of updates.

Study, study, study

The only way to get good at something is to work hard at it. The primary recommended resource for studying is Biology by Campbell and Reece, the Official textbook for USABO, endorsed by the national competition.

Take the exam

The exam will be administered in February, with specific dates communicated via Remind and at meetings. Before taking the test, make sure you get plenty of sleep and water.
